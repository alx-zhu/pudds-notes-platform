Design Rationale

Table stability:
The table layout never changes — same 7 columns, same cell structure. When sorting by a specific score category, only two things change: (1) row order, and (2) the sorted score gets emphasized (bolder label, slightly larger number) while other scores dim to ~45% opacity. This avoids any jarring column shifts or layout reflows.

Card emphasis pattern:
On cards, the same emphasis/dim logic applies. When sorting by Texture: the Texture bar row gets a bolder label, thicker bar (6px → 8px), and larger score number. The Overall number and other category rows dim to 40% opacity. When sorting by Overall (default), nothing is dimmed — all scores are equally visible.

Sort by Score — top of the popover:
Single-select chips: Overall (default), Taste, Texture, Color. Sits at the top, separated by a divider from filters. Chips are faster than a dropdown for 4 options.

Sort indicator in the active filters bar:
When a non-default sort is active, a muted grey chip ("Sort: Texture ↓") appears in the active filters bar. Informational only, not removable. Resets to Overall on "Clear all".

User story alignment:
• "Best chocolate benchtop puddings?" → Tap Chocolate + Benchtop, sorted by Overall
• "Best texture scores?" → Tap Texture sort, scan the emphasized scores top to bottom
• "Best vanilla taste?" → Tap Vanilla filter + Taste sort

Interaction model:
• Sort chips = single-select (tap one, replaces previous)
• Filter chips = multi-select (tap to toggle on/off)
• "Clear all" resets both sort (back to Overall) and all filters
• Sort is always active — there's no "no sort" state
• Emphasis is purely visual — no columns added/removed/rearranged
