Design v6: Thumbnail Photo
  • Key insight: the photo's purpose in this card is "visual context" (what does it look like?), not detailed inspection. A 56px thumbnail serves this purpose. Full photo is one click away in the existing modal.
  • Solves the aspect ratio problem — a 56px square thumbnail has zero layout impact. No side-by-side imbalance, no stacking dominance.
  • Photo + scores are paired in the same section. The thumbnail sits in a "log context" row above the score grid, alongside the log label. This answers "what am I looking at?" before showing "how good is it?"
  • Active timeline dot is now 18px (vs 14px), filled solid with a colored ring — much more visible contrast against unfilled outline dots.
  • Action hierarchy:
    • Footer: log-level actions (+ Add Log, Delete Log)
    • Thumbnail: click opens photo modal (edit photos there)
    • Eval pills: inline edit/delete icons appear on active/hover pill; + Add at end of row
  • Empty states: scores show em-dashes with 0-width bars; photo shows dashed placeholder thumbnail; no-logs shows centered CTA.
  • Inspired by: Vivino wine ratings, Untappd check-ins, Google Maps restaurant cards — all use small thumbnails alongside scores for visual context.
